# Tessera Time Model

Tessera is built on the idea that memory is not purely linear.

Most digital systems treat time as a single forward-moving timeline.  
Tessera instead treats time as something that both **moves forward and returns**.

Moments unfold sequentially, but patterns emerge across seasons, years, and traditions.

The Tessera time model captures both of these realities.

---

## Two Axes of Time

Tessera organizes memory across two conceptual axes.

### X-Axis: Local Time

Local time represents the lived progression of moments within a mosaic.

Examples:

- days during a trip
- weeks across a season
- evenings across a semester
- moments within a holiday

Local time forms the **narrative thread** of a mosaic.

Users experience this as a timeline of shared moments.

---

### Y-Axis: Cyclical Time

Cyclical time represents the recurrence of similar moments across longer spans.

Examples:

- Summer 2024
- Summer 2025
- Summer 2026

or

- annual gatherings
- recurring traditions
- seasonal rituals
- repeated trips

Cyclical time allows Tessera to reveal **patterns in shared life**.

---

## Mosaics

A **mosaic** is a bounded span of shared time.

It captures a chapter of life shared between a group of people.

Examples of mosaics:

- a summer with friends
- a semester in college
- a shared apartment
- a yearly camping trip
- a holiday season
- a major life transition

Within a mosaic, entries appear in chronological order along the local time axis.

---

## Tesserae

A **tessera** is a single moment contributed to a mosaic.

A tessera may contain:

- one or more photos
- a caption
- a timestamp
- an author

Tesserae form the tiles that make up the mosaic.

---

## Meta-Mosaics

Over time, patterns begin to appear across mosaics.

A **meta-mosaic** is a view that gathers moments across multiple mosaics based on shared time patterns.

Examples:

- all summers across several years
- every birthday celebration
- first snow of each winter
- opening nights of annual trips
- recurring traditions among friends

Meta-mosaics allow users to see **how shared life evolves across years**.

---

## Philosophy of Time in Tessera

Tessera assumes:

- life progresses linearly
- meaning emerges cyclically

Moments matter not only because they happened,
but because similar moments happen again.

By organizing memories across both sequence and recurrence,
Tessera becomes more than a photo archive.

It becomes a **structure of shared memory**.

---

## Design Implications

This time model leads to several design choices:

- mosaics are the primary containers of memory
- timelines show local progression of moments
- recurring views allow patterns across years
- users can revisit the same period across different cycles
- memories can be experienced both narratively and structurally

The system should emphasize **calm reflection rather than endless scrolling**.

---

## Summary

Chronology provides the surface.

Recurrence provides the deeper structure.

Tessera exists to reveal both.
