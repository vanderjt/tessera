# Tessera Design Baseline
Version: 0.1
Purpose: Record the foundational philosophy and structural decisions for the Tessera system.

This document captures the state of the concept before implementation begins.

---

# Core Vision

Tessera is a private space where small groups of friends share moments from their lives and assemble them into a living mosaic of memory.

The product emphasizes:

- intimacy
- presence
- reflection
- shared history

Tessera intentionally avoids engagement-driven mechanics common to social media platforms.

It is designed to feel calm, intentional, and human.

---

# Guiding Philosophy

Life moves forward, but meaning gathers in patterns.

Tessera allows friends to share small moments of their lives and gradually assemble them into a shared memory structure.

The platform prioritizes:

- meaningful connection over attention
- small groups over large networks
- reflection over immediacy

The goal is not to capture everything, but to preserve the moments that made someone think of their friends.

---

# Core Metaphor

Tessera is designed as a **sanctuary of shared memory**.

Users enter a quiet space containing mosaics that represent chapters of life shared with specific groups of people.

Within each mosaic, individuals contribute small moments (tesserae) that gradually form a collective memory artifact.

The interface should feel:

- quiet
- warm
- reflective
- intentional

---

# Primary Concepts

## Users

Individual participants in the system.

Users belong to circles and contribute tesserae to mosaics.

---

## Circles

Circles represent living social groups.

Examples:

- college friends
- roommates
- family
- travel group

Circle membership can change over time.

Circles exist primarily to make it easy for the same group of people to continue sharing moments together across new mosaics.

---

## Mosaics

A mosaic is a **chapter of shared time** belonging to a circle.

Examples:

- Summer 2026
- Apartment Life — Fall
- Italy Trip
- Winter Semester

Mosaics preserve the group of people who participated during that chapter.

Important rule:

Circle membership may change, but mosaic membership is fixed once the mosaic begins.

This preserves the historical authenticity of shared memory.

---

## Tesserae (Moments)

A tessera is a single shared moment.

A tessera may contain:

- optional photo(s)
- required short reflection
- author
- timestamp

Photos support the moment but are not required.

The reflection ensures moments feel intentional rather than transactional.

Examples:

"Saw this tiny bookstore today and thought of you."

"Finally tried the restaurant you recommended."

---

## Notes

Friends may respond privately to a tessera.

Each participant may send **one note per tessera**.

Notes are:

- private between responder and author
- not publicly visible in the mosaic
- editable but not repeatable

This prevents comment threads while preserving personal response.

---

# Posting Philosophy

Sharing a tessera should feel slightly more intentional than sending a text message but still lightweight.

Target effort:

~30–60 seconds.

Users should be able to share:

- small observations
- reflections
- mundane moments
- meaningful experiences

The system should avoid turning tesserae into performance content.

---

# Interaction Principles

Tessera deliberately avoids:

- likes
- reaction counts
- public metrics
- engagement competition

The only visible responses are private notes sent directly to the author.

The product encourages connection without social scoring.

---

# Entry Experience

When users open Tessera they enter the sanctuary.

The first screen emphasizes **people and spaces**, not a global feed.

Users see:

- presence signals (recent sharing or notes)
- the mosaics they belong to

Users choose which mosaic to enter.

There is no infinite cross-mosaic feed.

---

# Seasonal Mosaic Model

Circles generate mosaics across time.

When a mosaic becomes quiet or a new season begins, any member of the group may start the next mosaic.

Starting the next mosaic invites all members of the previous mosaic to continue.

This creates a sequence of mosaics across time.

Example:

Apartment Crew

Fall 2026  
Winter 2027  
Spring 2027  

Over time this forms a chronological archive of shared life chapters.

---

# Time Philosophy

Tessera organizes memory along two conceptual axes:

Local Time:
The chronological flow of moments within a mosaic.

Cyclical Time:
Recurring chapters across seasons or years.

This allows the system to later explore memory patterns across time.

Example future views:

- all summers
- recurring traditions
- the same week across multiple years

These features are not required for the first version.

---

# Text vs Photo

Photos are optional.

Text reflections are required.

The product is designed to remain meaningful even if some tesserae contain no photos.

Photos provide texture; reflections provide meaning.

---

# Cultural Intent

Tessera should produce a culture where people share:

- small daily moments
- reflections about their lives
- things that reminded them of their friends

Over time mosaics become artifacts of friendship and life chapters.

---

# Explicit Anti-Goals

Tessera should not become:

- a public social network
- an engagement platform
- a follower system
- a content feed
- a coordination tool for events

Those functions belong elsewhere.

Tessera is for memory and connection.

---

# Current Open Questions

The following design questions remain unresolved:

- exact visual structure of the mosaic interface
- the minimal feature set for the first launch
- notification philosophy
- circle creation and evolution mechanics
- meta-mosaic views across time

These will be addressed in future design baselines.
